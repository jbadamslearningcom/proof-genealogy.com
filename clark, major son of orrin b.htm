Notes
In 1870, Stephen and Sylvia Benjamin are living in Papineau Township, Iroquois County, Illinois.  They have three children living with them, Major Clark (Sylvia's son by Orrin Clark), Emma and Caroline Benjamin.

In 1880, Major Clark is living with Carrie Benjamin, William David, Jr., and Henry Wolf in Jackson Township, Newton County, Indiana.

In 1900, Major and May Clark are living in Concord Township, Iroquois County, Illinois.  They have four children living with them.

In 1910, Major and May Clark are living in Concord Township, Iroquois County, Illinois.  They have two children living with them, Fay and Frances.  They also have one grandchild living with them, Nita Smith.  (Nita is Faye's daughter.)

In 1920, N. Major and S. May Clarke are living in North Township, Lake County, Indiana.  They are in the household of Samuel and Gladys Clarke.  Also living with them is Vivian, age 9.

The 1930 census shows Susan I. Clark living with her brother A.H. Denton in Concord Township, Iroquois County, Illinois.
Major Clark is not living with them; the census lists her as "widowed."

The 1930 census shows a George Clark, born in Illinois in 1862, living as an inmate in the Lake County Poor Farm.
