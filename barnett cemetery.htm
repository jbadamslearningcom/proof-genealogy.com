Barnett Cemetery
 

 

 

 

Barnett Cemetery is located near the Iroquois River, east of Chebanse, Illinois, in Kankakee County.  The name of the cemetery comes from James Barnett, who gave up two acres of his land for the cemetery, and continued to farm the land around it.  James Barnett is buried in the cemetery, as are several members of the Barnett family.

People from the Sugar Island area were buried there up until the 1910s.  By the 1930's, the cemetery had fallen into a state of neglect.  The following clipping appeared in the Iroquois County Times newspaper in 1934, reprinted from the Chebanse Herald.

<clipping>

Despite the call to action, the cemetery continued to decline, eventually becoming overgrown with trees and brush.

The cemetery is on private property and is difficult to access.  So to save you the trouble of trekking out to the Brown Hill Cemetery, Proof-Genealogy.com is proud to present you with the opportunity to visit its graves virtually.
