1861 letter from Harrison Morrical to Margaret and Sarah Bryant


 

Date:  The letter is believed to have been written on March 24, 1861.
From: To:
Harrison
Morrical
1835-1912

Margaret
Bryant
1843-1903

 Sarah Ann
Bryant
1848-1932
At the time of the letter, Harrison was 25 years old, single, and living with his mother and younger siblings in Kankakee County, Illinois.  The letter was written to his cousins, Margaret and Sarah Bryant.  Margaret was 17 years old; Sarah was about 12.   The Bryant sisters were living in Brook, Indiana; the girls may or may not have been living with their mother, Malinda.
  Dear Cousins,

I wish you could have bin with us this spring for I think that you would have enjoyed yourselves very well.  Cousin Francis and Elen Morrical is down from Laporte Co.  They have bin here to or three weeks.  They talk of going home but I think they want to go for some time yet.  They are very lively.  Frank is a good boy.  He taught school last winter and writing school, allso.  He is a going to start to seminary school the first of May.  Elias and probably myself are a-going a long with Him at Valparaso, Porter County, Ind.

We had a party at Alexander Miller's last week.  I expect Cousin Daniel is married to Ander Harter's Wife's sister.  Daniel was here all last winter but he is at Laporte now.

Grandfather got a letter from Uncle Andrew Morrical and they are all well.  Uncle Washington's Wife the Widow was married.  Her and her man has parted.  She is a-living in Tippecanoe Town, Marshall County, Ind.  Her and William and the little girl is a-living together, gess is working oute.  Isick is on the County.  Stephen is [unreadable].  Calvin C. Morrical and his wife have parted.  His wife is at home and he is in [Minnesota].

You was telling me that Uncle Samuel had bought a place but didn't tell what direction it was from the old farm, or how much land.

Pleas pay us a visit one and all.

Margaret Elen and Sarah Ann

Dear Cousins I would like to see you

Write Soon

I remain Yours

  

Footnotes

1

 Francis Morrical and Ellen Morrical were cousins, the son and daughter of Harrison's uncle Jacob Morrical.  They lived in La Porte County, Indiana.  

2

 Elias is Harrison's brother.  

3

 Alexander Miller is Harrison's brother-in-law; the husband of Harrison's sister Pheby.  

4

 Daniel Morrical is another cousin, the eldest son of Harrison's uncle Andrew Morrical.  

5

 

 "Grandfather" is John Morrical, who was living with his daughter-in-law Sarah Morrical (Clark's widow) and her children, including Harrison.  

6

 Andrew Morrical is Harrison's uncle, who lived in Jay County, Indiana.  

7

 

 Washington Morrical died in 1958.  His widow Mary married Thomas Malone in Marshall County Indiana in 1859.  

8

 

 Washington had five children, four of whom are mentioned in this letter: William Morrical, Isaac Morrical, Finley Stephen Morrical, and Mary Ellen Morrical ("the little girl").  It is not known why Harrison failed to mention his other cousin, Jesse Morrical.  

9

 Calvin C. Morrical was another cousin; the eldest son of Harrison's uncle Jacob Morrical.  

10

 "Uncle Samuel" is Samuel Broderick, the second husband of Harrison's aunt, Malinda Morrical.  Samuel Broderick was a step-father to Margaret and Sarah Bryant.
