Notes

In the 1820s, Case Wadley was involved in the fur trade headquartered in
Danville, Illinois.  He worked for Gurdon Hubbard, a well known fur trader who
established a trail between Danville and Chicago that came to be known as the
"Hubbard Trace."  It was in Vermillion County that he married his first wife.
(Danville is in Vermillion County.)

In the 1830s, Case Wadley moved his family to Boubonnais, Illinois and he got involved with the Bourbonnais Trading Post.  After his first wife died, he started a farm just south of Waldron (present day Aroma Park) in 1833.  Some time in the 1830s he married Mrs. Maria Boardman.  They had eight (or more) children together.
