Notes

 

After Lloyd's mother (Nancy Emeline Mathews) died, his father, Robert Goodfellow, thought it was best for him to have a mother since he was so young.  Lloyd was sent to live in Parham, Ontario, with his uncle David Goodfellow and aunt Lucretia.   When Robert Goodfellow married Jane Hannah Bertrim in 1871, he contacted his brother to let him know that he wanted Lloyd back.  David couldn't part with him, and Robert allowed Lloyd to stay in Parham.

  
 

This photo of  Daniel Lloyd Goodfellow was taken in 1891 in Michigan.

  
 

Obituary for Daniel Lloyd Goodfellow

Daniel Lloyd Goodfellow of Westport in the Parson's Nursing Home, Smith Falls, on Thursday Oct. 7, in his 86th year.  He was born in Illinois, U.S.A. on July 16, 1868, the son of Mr. and Mrs.  Robert Goodfellow.  His mother died when he was a few months old and he was brought up by his Uncle, David Goodfellow, of Parham.

In his early twenties Mr. Goodfellow resided in the United States for a few years but later returned to Canada where he was employed as a commercial traveler.

In 1899, he opened a furniture and funeral business in Westport. The following year he was married to Mary McCullough of Lansdowne, who predeceased him in 1940.  He is survived by three sons, Dr. F. Goodfellow, a practicing physician in Westport; W. R. Goodfellow, who operates the business founded by his father and H. D. L. Goodfellow, B. A. who is a psychologist with the Ontario Department of Health at Orillia.  Four grandchildren and one sister, Mrs. Ida Posson of Ulysses, Nebraska, also survive.

During more than half a century of residence in Westport, Mr. Goodfellow gave much of his time and effort to the welfare to the community.  He belonged to the United Church and was a member of the Board of Stewards.  He was also a member of the Public School Board, serving for many years as chairman of the board. When he retired he was presented with a parchment, expressing appreciation for his almost 30 years of continuous service. 

Funeral services was conducted by Mr. Lorne Keays, B.A., Westport, assisted by Rev. R. A. McRae Newboro. Interment was made in the United Church Cemetery, Westport.

[Source: Goodfellow Scrapbook, Westport]
