Charles Copke & Sophia Wagner

Karl Kopke married Sophia Wagner Hummel in 1866 in Germany.   At the time, Sophia had one son, Henry Hummel, from a previous marriage.  When the family came to America in 1872, Karl Kopke changed his name to Charles Copke.  The family name also appears as Koepke.

Charles and Sophia came to America from Germany with their children.  It is believed
that they lived near the Illinois-Wisconsin border for several years before moving to
Otto Township, Kankakee County, Illinois.  Other family members came with them
from Germany, but the names of most of these relatives have been lost.

Charles's father was Fred Kopke; it is not known when
he died, but he has a gravestone in Chebanse, Illinois.

Letters indicate that Charles had a sister who lived in Roscoe, Illinois,
a small town on the Illinois-Wisconsin border.  Her name is not known.

This photo shows Charles and Sophia (on the right) with another couple.
 The photo is labeled "Charles and Sophie Copke, and Sophie's sister and husband."
