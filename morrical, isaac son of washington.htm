Notes
In 1850, Isaac Morrical is living with his parents, Washington and Mary Morrical, in Newcastle Township, Fulton County, Indiana.  He has two older brothers living with him.

After Washington's death in 1858, the family split up.  It is believed that Isaac became a ward of the county, residing at the county's "poor farm" asylum.  He would have been about 12 years old at the time.

In 1880, Isaac Morrical is listed as an "inmate" in the county house run by Francis Jaysox in Polk Towhship, Marshall County, Indiana.

According to county asylum records, Isaac was kicked by a horse on August 8, 1910 and died on August 9, 1910.  He was buried in the asylum cemetery, Row 6, Lot 2.  His body was claimed by his brother Finley.
