Photos
 

This photo of the Morrical siblings was taken around the turn of the century.  Seated
in front:    Alamanda, Harrison, and Pheby.  Standing in back:  Lewis and Elias.
The photo pasted in the upper  left corner is that of the eldest brother, Adam.
 The photo in the upper right is believed to be that of the youngest sister Sarah.

 

Clark and Sarah Morrical had been dead for over fifty
years when their descendants began holding family
reunions.  The first Morrical family reunion was held
in 1919.  Only two of Clark and Sarah Morrical's
children were alive at the time -- Lewis and Alamanda.

Above:  Group photo from the first Morrical family
reunion in 1919.  The reunion was held in rural
Herscher, Illinois.  Forty people were in attendance.

Right:  Siblings Alamanda and Lewis in 1919.
Alamanda was 68 years old; Lewis was 71.

Below:  Group photo from the Morrical family
reunion in 1920.  The reunion was held at Elmer
Morrical's home in rural Irwin, Illinois.
Ninety-one  people were in attendance.

 

Left:  Newspaper clipping describing
the Morrical family reunion in 1934. 

Below:  Newspaper clipping describing
the Morrical family reunion in 1937.
