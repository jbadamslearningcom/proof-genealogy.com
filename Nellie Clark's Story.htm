Notes

        Case Wadley worked for Gurdon Hubbard in the fur trade at Danville.  Then Wadley moved to the Bourbonnais Trading Post.  Wadley’s wife died and he opened a farm just south of Waldron in 1833 and married Mrs. Maria Boardman.  Maria was eleven years younger than Case.  They had six children plus his son, Jasper, which made seven. 

        Meanwhile, Case’s brother, Sylvester, who was born in New York, and wife, Elizabeth, born in Pennsylvania and five year old than Sylvester, were still in Ohio where they go married.  They had Mary Margaret in about 1837.  They then moved to Indiana and had their second daughter, Sylvia about a year and half later.

        They moved their family to Illinois to be near his brother Case who was by Aroma Park on a farm.  Sylvester is a cooper by trade so he may have lived in the nearby settlement by the Iroquois River.  This was part of Iroquois County at this time until 1854 when Kankakee County was formed.

        About the same time, John and Sarah Clark arrived and bought 160 acres next to the Beaver Creek Cemetery then known as the Jones Cemetery.  They had three sons and a daughter.  The daughter Mahala remained in Indiana.  Sons William, Bradford, and Orrin came to Papineau township which became part of Iroquois County.  By the 1850’s, Sylvia Wadley and O. B. Clark who lived 6 miles south were acquainted and on January 1st, 1856, 18 year old Sylvia and 27 year old Orrin were married.  In 1858, they had a daughter named Lydia.  Father-in-law John Clark died suddenly that same year.  The next year O.B. and Sylvia have another daughter born to them.  They call her Alice.  At this same time, mother-in-law Sarah is living with them.   In the winter of 60-61 baby Alice died.  Sylvia had been pregnant since last summer.  Her baby boy, who they named Major, was born on April 22nd of 1861.  Twelve days later, O. B. Clark, the new baby’s father, died.  He was 32.

Stephen Benjamin rented the 160 acres of Clark farm ground through Nelson Bancroft, a 33 year old farmer from just south in Martinton township who was the administrator for O. B. Clark’s estate.

        Sylvia apparently liked the new farmer for she married Stephen in 1863 and they continued to farm the Clark place.  The next year Stephen and Sylvia have their first daughter that they named Emaline.   The following year seven-year-old Lydia, a daughter to O. B. Clark died on the 2nd of July 1865.  Now we have in the household Sylvia and Stephen with O. B.’s son Major and their daughter Emaline.  Mother-in-law Sarah or Sally moved out to her son William’s house for a year or so after O. B. died.  She also lived in Indiana with her daughter Mahala for three years then she lived again in Papineau township with her 3rd son Bradford Clark.

        In 1867 Stephen and Sylvia have their 2nd child a daughter named Caroline.

        The family feud is in full progress by now and on the 8th of October, 1870, Sally Clark sued her daughter-in-law Sylvia Wadley Clark Benjamin for support from the Clark farm, which she claimed she had not received since her son O. B. died.

        Like most court cases, it lingered on and on.  Before its conclusion, Stephen Benjamin, a 38-year-old man died suddenly on the 10th of September 1872.  The following year in November, Judge Chamberlin made a decision for the case.

        Before the case was settled, Sylvia married David Brown on June 11th, 1873.  A month or so after the case is over, 1874 begins and is an eventful year.

        Nine months after Sylvia married David Brown, he too died in March of 1874.  Two months after husband David’s death, Sally Clark, mother-in-law from the first marriage dies in May 1874.  The following month Sylvia sues her son Major Clark now 13 as a result of the court decision and the deaths of O. B. and his mother Sally.  The results of this case are that Sylvia got one third of the land, the house to live in, and $1000 for Lydia, the living daughter, at the time O. B. died.

        Sylvia’s mother Elizabeth died _____.  Her father moves in with his brother Case Wadley by Aroma Park.  A new generation is about to begin as Sylvia’s family now numbers four children, Major Clark from her first marriage, Emiline, Caroline, and son Levi from the marriage to Stephen Benjamin.  On Dec. 31st, News Year’s Eve, 1878, daughter Emiline, who now calls herself Emily, married Horace J. Clark.  She must have listed her age as 16 to the Justice of the Peace who married them when in reality, she was 14 years old at that time.  Her new husband Horace is 9 or 10 years older than Sylvia and a first cousin to her half brother Major Clark.  He was born in Indiana to Wm. B. and Sarah Ann Clark and had 6 siblings.  Wm. B. lived in Papineau township.  By 1880 Sylvia is living on a farm with William Dunning, Chancy Smith and his 8-year-old son George.  Dunning, who was retired, owns the farm, on the North edge of Iroquois, that once belonged to Gurdon Hubbard who used it as a fur trading post at what was then called Bunkwum.  Smith was farming the ground and Sylvia was keeping house for the men.

        She must have liked this farmer too since she married him on the 2nd of March 1880.

        Nearby, daughter Emily, her husband Horace, and 8-month-old baby boy Sylvester, probably named after Grandpa Sylvester Wadley, lived in Concord township.  Meanwhile about 25 miles to the northwest in Newton County Indiana and Jackson township, Sylvia’s children Carrie and Major are living with William David and Henry Wolf.  Carrie is 13 and keeping house, Henry is 16 and Major is 19.  William David who is 23 is the son of William and Jemima David whose daughter Eliza married Sid Benjamin, brother of Stephen Benjamin, Sylvia’s 2nd husband.  Stephen’s father Elijah died in 1882.  That same year Emma and Horace have their second son Arthur.  Horace and Emma moved to Aroma Park where he was known as the village blacksmith.  By 1893 Emma, calling herself Nellie, was having a daughter, Gertrude, running a business in Champaign and getting a divorce from Horace J. Clark.  By 1893, Nellie moved her business to 367 S. West Avenue, Kankakee.  Former husband Horace Clark married Elva Jacobus in 1896.  She died in 1918 and he in 1920 and they are buried in Aroma Park.

        Arthur and Gertrude live with Grandmother Sylvia Smith in 1900.  Sylvia died in Kankakee on  _________.  In 1918, Nell’s children, Arthur and Gertrude were sent to Joliet Prison for larceny.  Gertie worked with her mother and was arrested many times.  Levi Benjamin had his problems with bribery, arson, insurance fraud and illegal liquor.  Nellie died on the 28th of April 1942.  Her son Sylvester died in 1960.

        Marie Clark who was born on the 2nd of May 1888 in Austria, died on the 30th of November 1968.  Cause of death is listed as extreme obesity.  She was Nell’s daughter-in-law.

        I’m sure there is more such as grandparent’s deaths, Wadleys and Benjamins and many cousins from Sylvia’s sister, O. B.’s brothers, Stephen’s family and information from Aroma Cemetery.

Emma Benjamin was known around
the Kankakee area as Nellie Clark,
 the notorious bordello madame.

After her death, her body was placed
in a grand mausoleum in the Beaver
Creek Cemetery.  Rumors that the
 tomb held some of her wealth caused
caused vandals to break in an disturb
her remains.  Authorities removed her
 body -- it is believed to be buried
 nearby in an unmarked spot.

Today the mausoleum stands empty.
